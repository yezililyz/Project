Flagship Project

Proof of Match: AI-Powered Deal-Ready Networking

1. Problem Statement

Traditional event networking relies on shallow keyword signals. An investor and a founder might share a "DeFi" tag but have deep mismatches in funding stage, mandate, or operational readiness, resulting in low-signal conversations that waste high-value time.Event organizers feel the churn pressure and lack of insight brining sponsor ROI to YoY revenue growth signal

2. Research & Insights

Through interviews, LinkedIn how‑tos, and founder playbooks, I identified that deal-ready meetings require complementarity (offer vs. need) rather than just similarity. I mapped JTBD for three personas: Investors (thesis-fit pipeline), Founders (strategic capital), and Regulators (policy engagement). And researched the current gap of existing tooling for capital networking organizer complaining the ROI is fuzzy and organizers struggle to attribute meetings to business results.

3. Solution & Process

Architected a 6-agent AI pipeline with fact signals that enriches raw data, extracts traction facts, and scores pairs across 7 independent dimensions: Persona Fit, Complementary Value, Intent Overlap, Sector Overlap, Stage Fit, Credibility, and Non-Obviousness matching.

4. Impact & Metrics

Delivered an intelligence engine that generates prioritized "Match Cards" with natural language reasoning. The system successfully surfaces non-obvious connections—detecting when a fintech founder and a healthcare regulator are solving the same protocol challenges.

5. Reflection

The core challenge was balancing semantic flexibility with deterministic filters, a classical tradepff between deterministic and probabilistic pipeline in traditional ML and LLM outcomes. By separating 'who you are' from 'what you want' in the embedding space, we avoided the 'echo chamber' effect of similarity-only matching. Next iterations would focus on incorporating real-time feedback loops from scheduled meeting outcomes

Go1: Retention via Content Intelligence

$1.7M ARR Retained
+35% Playability Lift

1. Problem: Technical failures and poor metadata were blocking users from accessing curated content, leading to churn in the enterprise sector.Fragmented event tracking across mobile and desktop platforms made it impossible to isolate true activation/engagement signals from noise for 8M+ users

2. Research: Conducted behavioral funnel analysis and synthesized qualitative insights with quantitative engagement data. Analyzed search-to-play rates as an activation proxy, identifying friction points in the content consumption funnel.

3. Solution: Drove core product growth by building an LLM-powered metadata enrichment pipeline and rebuilding the learner activation and engagement layer. Architected global event schemas that cleanly isolated true learner and content-partner activation signals from noise, and redesigned the content partner upload workflow to unlock higher-quality supply and faster time-to-value.

4. Impact: Yielded +25% search discoverability and +15% content consumption, retaining $1.7M in ARR.

5. Reflection: Found that "content playability" is often a data-integrity issue rather than a UX issue. Automated metadata enrichment is the only way to scale curation for enterprise-grade learning libraries.

Dailymotion: User insight led to platform revamp & Global Taxonomy Redesign

+58% Search Views
+25% Revenue Lift

1. Problem: For new mobile users, search was the dominant entry point (90% of sessions) but only 60% converted into a video play, signaling weak relevance and a leaky first-session experience. Inconsistent tracking between mobile and desktop obscured the impact of UI changes and search improvements for a 400M+ user base.

2. Research: Ran structured user interviews, layered on top of funnel and cohort analysis, to understand how Gen Z users searched, discovered, and decided to watch. Used this to pinpoint friction in the watch page, search ranking, and topic understanding.

3. Solution: Designed and rolled out a unified Amplitude event model and dashboards that connected acquisition sources to activation, engagement, and retention. Led a combined effort on watch page UX revamp, search provider migration, and Google NLP–powered topic annotation to enable semantic search, while embedding an A/B testing and tracking framework into the product development lifecycle.

4. Impact: Increased overall engagement by 17%, improved search-to-play conversion by 25%, and drove a 50% uplift in views originating from search, alongside a 20% reduction in infrastructure costs.

5. Reflection: Durable growth depends on clean taxonomy and reliable instrumentation. Once events and cohorts are trustworthy, product and growth decisions shift from intuition to repeatable experimentation.
