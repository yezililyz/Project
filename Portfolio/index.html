Flagship Project · 2026

Proof of Match: AI-Powered Deal-Ready Networking

Traditional event networking relies on shallow keyword signals. Proof of Match redesigns the matching model from the ground up — from similarity to complementarity.

1. Problem Statement

An investor and founder might share a "DeFi" tag but have deep mismatches in funding stage, mandate, or operational readiness — resulting in low-signal conversations that waste high-value time. Event organisers feel the churn pressure and lack of insight threatening sponsor ROI and YoY revenue. I wanted to design a system that makes matching more intentional, transparent, and easier to act on — without turning it into a shallow networking app.

2. Research & Insights

Through interviews, LinkedIn how-tos, and founder playbooks, I identified that deal-ready meetings require complementarity (offer vs. need) rather than just similarity. I mapped Jobs-to-be-Done for three personas: Investors (thesis-fit pipeline), Founders (strategic capital), and Regulators (policy engagement) — and surfaced the gap that existing tooling can't attribute meetings to business results.

3. Solution & Architecture

Architected a 6-agent AI pipeline that enriches raw profiles, extracts traction signals, and scores pairs across 7 independent dimensions: Persona Fit, Complementary Value, Intent Overlap, Sector Overlap, Stage Fit, Credibility, and Non-Obviousness matching signals. The pipeline generates prioritised "Match Cards" with natural language reasoning, surfacing non-obvious connections.

4. Reflection

The core challenge was balancing semantic flexibility with deterministic filters — the classical trade-off between probabilistic and deterministic pipelines. By separating "who you are" from "what you want" in the embedding space, we avoided the echo-chamber effect of similarity-only matching. Next iterations would incorporate real-time feedback loops from meeting outcomes.

Go1 · B2B SaaS · Enterprise Learning

Retention via Content Intelligence

$1.7M ARR retained
+35% Playability lift
+25% Search discoverability
Problem

Go1 had a vast B2B learning catalog, but learners and admins struggled to find the right content, leading to low engagement and underused contracts. Technical failures and poor metadata blocked users from curated content, driving churn. Fragmented event tracking made it impossible to isolate true activation signals from noise across 8M+ users.

Research

Partnered with CS and sales to collect qualitative feedback on L&D curation pain. Conducted quantitative funnel analysis using search-to-play rates as an activation proxy, identifying the specific friction points blocking content consumption.

Solution

Built an LLM-powered metadata enrichment pipeline; rebuilt the learner activation layer; architected global Amplitude event schemas that cleanly isolated true activation signals; redesigned the content partner upload workflow to unblock high-quality supply.

Reflection

"Content playability" is often a data-integrity issue, not a UX issue. Automated metadata enrichment is the only way to scale curation for enterprise-grade learning libraries.

Dailymotion · B2C · 400M+ Users

Platform Revamp & Global Taxonomy Redesign

+58% Search views
+35% Revenue lift
−20% Infra costs
Problem

~90% of new mobile users started with search, but barely 60% led to a video play in the same session. Inconsistent mobile/desktop tracking made it impossible to tie UI changes to downstream impact across a 400M+ user base.

Research

Combined structured user interviews with funnel and cohort analysis, segmented by new vs. returning users and acquisition channel. Traced where users were leaking along search-to-play, session depth, and repeat-view patterns — surfacing three core friction points.

Solution

Designed and deployed a unified Amplitude event model and cross-platform dashboards. Led a cross-functional initiative to revamp watch-page UX, migrate to a stronger search provider, and introduce Google NLP-powered topic annotation. All changes embedded in A/B testing infra for rigorous validation.

Reflection

Durable growth depends on clean taxonomy and reliable instrumentation. By reframing Dailymotion's rebrand as a mobile-first, Gen Z challenge and using search-to-play as the primary activation lens, we aligned UX, relevance, and data infrastructure around one narrative.

BlaBlaCar · Marketplace · 9 Markets

Growth Lifecycle Experiments & Multi-Touch Attribution

+15% Return trip bookings
+20% User activation
+30% Marketing efficiency
Problem

Return-trip booking rates were lagging and marketing spend was inefficient across 9 markets — performance measured on last-click attribution masked true multimodal behaviour and underestimated BlaBlaCar's role in the journey.

Research

Proxy analysis revealed 9% of users booked outbound and return on different modalities. Once controlled, the 12-point apparent gap in return bookings shrank to 3 points — reframing the question from "fix BlaBlaBus" to "increase any return trip on BlaBlaCar by 3pp."

Solution

Architected a lifecycle experiments system with clearer return-trip prompts, cohort logic by outbound modality, and Braze event-triggered journeys tuned to booking timing windows. Implemented a Markov-chain multi-touch attribution model, redistributing credit across the full journey. Treated tracking as a blocking dependency — regression-tested the entire stack before launch.

Reflection

Transitioning from simple re-engagement to event-triggered activation requires tight coordination between marketing and product data layers — and a willingness to redefine the problem before building the solution.
